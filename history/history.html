History of Ha Noi

Name

Ha Noi had many official and unofficial names throughout history.

During the Chinesee occupation of Vietnam, it was known first as Long Biên, then Tống Bình and Long Đỗ Long Biên later gave its name to famed Long Bien Bridge, built during French colonial times, and more recently to a new district to the east of the Red River.

In 866, It was turned into a citadel and named Đại La This gave it the nickname La Thành (net citadel). Both Đại La and La Thành are names of major streets in modern Ha Noi nowadays.

When Lý Thái Tổ established the capital in the area in 1010, It was named Thăng Long, later became the name of a major bridge on the highway linking the city center to Nội Bài airport and Thăng Long Boulevard expressway in the southwest of the city center.

1. Pre-Thăng Long period

Hà Nội has been inhabited since at least 3000 BC. Tống Bình seems the first name of it named by the Liu Song Dynasty after they annexed Âu Lạc Kingdom by the 5th century, It later became a commandery. By the year of 679, the Tang dynasty ruled the area and set Tống Bình as its capital. In order to defeat the people's uprisings, in the later half of the 8th century, the Tang dynasty built La Thành. In the earlier of the 9th century, it was futher built up and called Kim Thành - the largest citadel of ancient Hanoi at the time.

2. Thăng Long, Đông Đô, Đông Quan, Đông Kinh

In 1010, Lý Thái Tổ, the first ruler of the Lý dynasty, moved the capital of Đại Việt to the cite of Đại La Citadel. He claimed to have seen a dragon ascending the Red River, so he decided rename the site Thăng Long (Soaring Dragon). In 1397, the capital was moved to Thanh Hóa, then known as Tây Đô (Western Capital), Thăng Long then became Đông Đô (Eastern Capital). In 1408, Minh dynasty attacted and occupied Vietnam, changing Đông Đô to Đông Quan. In 1408, The Chinese Minh dynasty was defeated by Lê Lợi who later found Lê dynasty and renamed Đông Quan to Đông Kinh (Eastern Captital).

3. During Nguyễn Dynasty and the French colonial period


People in Hà Nội, 1884


When the Nguyễn dynasty was established in 1802, Gia Long King moved the capital to Huế. Thăng Long was no longer the capital, so aiming to reduce the sentiment of Lê dynasty and because "dragon" was usually used as a symbol of the power of Emperors, if the King was no longer in Thăng Long, it was no longer called Thăng Long anymore, Minh Mạng renamed Thăng Long to Hà Nội (inside the rivers). As Hà Nội was located in the protectorate of Tonkin, became the capital of French Indochina after 1887.
